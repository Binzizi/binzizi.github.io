Biography

I pursued my PhD at the Laboratory for Public Management & Policy (LaMP), under the supervision of Prof. Richard M. WALKER. I was awarded the Dissertation Fellowship in July 2024 by the China-America Association for Public Affairs (CAAPA). I received my degree in October 2024 and took a postdoctoral position at the Centre for Public Affairs and Law (CPAL) at the City University of Hong Kong.

My research primarily focuses on behavioral public administration and computational social sciences, with interests in citizen-government relationships and public e-service delivery. My expertise includes experimental vignette methodology and machine learning approach.
